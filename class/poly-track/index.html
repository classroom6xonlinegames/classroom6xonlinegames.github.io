    PolyTrack

    PolyTrack is a racing game that combines fast driving with track creation. Players control a customizable car and race on tracks that can either be pre-built or designed by the players themselves. The game uses a clean low-poly style and includes loops, ramps, jumps, and tight turns where precise driving and quick lap times are essential.

    Inspired by time-trial racing games, PolyTrack focuses on beating the clock and improving your best time on different tracks. Players can compete against their previous records while also exploring tracks created by other members of the PolyTrack community.

    How to Play PolyTrack

    Start by selecting a track. You can choose from existing courses or design your own using the built-in level editor. Once the track is ready, pick your car and customize its appearance to give it a unique look.

    After entering the race, your goal is to finish the track as quickly as possible. Drive through ramps, turns, and obstacles while maintaining control of your vehicle. Careful steering and accurate timing are essential if you want to achieve the fastest lap times.

    Controls

    Driving Controls

    • Accelerate – Press W or the Up Arrow key
    • Brake / Reverse – Press S or the Down Arrow key
    • Steer Left – Press A or the Left Arrow key
    • Steer Right – Press D or the Right Arrow key
    • Restart Track – Press R or Enter to reset the run quickly

    Editor Controls

    • Place Track Elements – Use the Left Mouse Button to position track pieces
    • Rotate Camera View – Hold the Middle Mouse Button and move the mouse
    • Move Camera Position – Hold the Right Mouse Button and move the mouse
    • Zoom In / Out – Scroll the Mouse Wheel
    • Adjust Element Height – Hold Shift and scroll the mouse wheel, or press Z and C
    • Rotate Selected Element – Press R or Spacebar
    • Delete Element – Press X
    • Test Drive Track – Press T to instantly try your track design

    Track Editor and Community Creations

    PolyTrack includes a built-in level editor that allows players to design their own racing circuits. This editor makes it possible to create anything from simple straight tracks to complex courses with large jumps and creative layouts.

    Players can share their designs with others, which means new tracks are constantly being created by the community.

    Time Trials and Ghost Racing

    When selecting a track, you can see the best lap times achieved by verified players. This allows you to compare your performance and attempt to beat their records.

    A unique feature in PolyTrack is asynchronous competition. You can select players from the leaderboard and race against their recorded runs. Their cars appear as “ghost” vehicles during the race, letting you follow their route and try to finish faster. You can also race against your own ghost to improve your previous best time.

    Tips to Improve Your Lap Time

    Getting faster lap times is less about driving aggressively and more about reducing small mistakes that slow you down. Paying attention to control and positioning can make a big difference.

    • Brake earlier before corners – Braking too late often causes the car to slide wide and lose speed.
    • Land jumps straight – If you land at an angle, you’ll need to correct your direction, which wastes valuable time.
    • Take tight lines through turns – Driving a shorter path through corners helps you maintain speed and finish faster.
    • Approach platforms directly – Hitting platforms from the side can slow your car or interrupt your momentum.
    • Stay lower on wall rides – A lower position on wall sections makes it easier to exit smoothly and maintain control.
    • Restart quickly after mistakes – Repeating difficult sections helps you learn the track and improve your time faster.
